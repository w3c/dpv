<ghurlbot> Issue 368 [FIX]: Typo on Reputational Risks concepts codes starting with Lose (by csarven)

JulianFlake: (what's the change) -- Lose is a verb, Loss is a noun. What is the common practice? What do we use?

HarshPandit: apparently we use nouns

JulianFlake: Option 1 is better (LossCredibility) as this saydpv 2.2 draft reviews there is Loss and then what it is about. It sounds werid in terms of phrasing. LossOfCredibility is not among the options.

DelaramGolpayegani: this concept is a consequence or an impact, so it would be a Loss of Credibility

HarshPandit: We can have LossCredibility as the term and Loss of Credibility in the label

DelaramGolpayegani: so we follow our best practice for consistency between labels and terms (use of "of" here), and are we using the verb form that implies the event or the noun.

HarshPandit: we should put this on the wiki (my grammar is not good enough) https://github.com/w3c/dpv/wiki/Contribution-Guide (please help)

JulianFlake: When we have a rule that we skip "of", "in", "at" etc. in the term while adding that in the label; then its not weird to read the label. But its still weird for people who see the terms for the first time.

DelaramGolpayegani: or if we have the option that we can avoid these in the first place (e.g. by going with CredibilityLoss) then we go with that as a preference
