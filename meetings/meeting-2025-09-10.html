<ghurlbot> Issue 385 Align DPV with CRDM AI concepts (by coolharsh55)

HarshPandit: The Publications Office mentioned that they curated this list with definitions and links to literature as state of the art, and that these would be formal taxonomies. Therefore I propose that we align the DPV's taxonomies to these e.g. by adding concepts that are missing.

HarshPandit: I have also mentioned the DPV work to them in the webinar, and asked about plans to model purposes or intended uses -- but it was mentioned that there are no plans because they don't know how to mention what should be part of these concepts. So we are ahead of the curve here, which is a good opportunity for us, and we should take advantage of it.

Proportionality (post-meeting)

post-meeting session with participants HarshPandit and StratisKoulierakis

StratisKoulierakis: Proportionality is a concept which is present in EU law outside the GDPR, so it is well established. We should model is as states containing Proportionate or Disproportionate (confirmed term occurs in case law). In addition, we will need ProportinalityAssessment and NecessityAssessment as concepts as well.

HarshPandit: How do we express the relation between these?

StratisKoulierakis: Proportionality involves steps 1) suitability assessment -- is the activity going to bring the desired effect? 2) is this activity necessary or are there alternative and better approaches? Further concept Strict Proportionality also called the balancing exercise i.e. to balance the rights of person and detriments to them with the intended activity. So proportionality assessments have three components: 1) SuitabilityAssessment 2) NecessityAssessment 3) BalancingAssessment. Proportionality comes up e.g. when the controller has legitimate interests, then its required to have a proportionality concept.

ACTION: Harsh to model concepts as per Stratis' suggestion for discussion.
