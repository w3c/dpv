MyTerms launch

IainHenderson: IEEE 7012-2025 standard for machine-readable privacy terms ; launching on 28th January F2F in London and as a webinar (https://mytermswebinar.eventbrite.com for online attendance; https://mytermslondon.eventbrite.com for in-person in London, UK)

IainHenderson: event is free; it mentions DPV for implementation

website: https://myterms.info/

IainHenderson: MyTerms is about privacy policies (terms/contracts) being standardised and written from the perspective of the individual. Two types of agreements: one is about the relationship e.g. consumer and the other is about donating data e.g. altruism. DPV is used for definitions and expressing requirements in machine-readable form.

IainHenderson: the standard's implementation will be done by MyTerms Alliance which will have Customers Commons and MyData; there will be a seminar in Brussels at the end of February

JanLindquist: What's the relation between MyTerms and 27560 consent records?

IainHenderson: Some of the information is common; though MyTerms is about contracts rather than consent but the privacy implications are relevant;

JanLindquist: Is 7012 freely available?

IainHenderson: Not yet, but we are trying to have it, or to put the key implementation details via MyTerms

JanLindquist: co-editor of 27560 (with Harsh) which is now about all legal bases rather than just consent so it could be helpful to consider. And 27560 is freely available.
