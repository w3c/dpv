Abstract

The LEGAL extension extends the Data Privacy Vocabulary (DPV) Specification to represent laws, authorities, and other legal concepts in various jurisdictions.

The canonical URL for LEGAL extension is https://w3id.org/dpv/legal, the namespace is https://w3id.org/dpv/legal#, the suggested prefix is legal, and this document along with source and releases are available at https://github.com/w3c/dpv.

DPV Specifications: The [DPV] is the core specification that is extended by specific extensions. A [PRIMER] introduces the concepts and modelling of DPV specifications, and [GUIDES] describe application of DPV for specific applications and use-cases. The Search Index page provides a searchable hierarchy of all concepts. The Data Privacy Vocabularies and Controls Community Group (DPVCG) develops and manages these specifications through GitHub. For meetings, see the DPVCG calendar.

The peer-reviewed article "Data Privacy Vocabulary (DPV) - Version 2.0" (2024) describes the current state of DPV and extensions from version 2.0 onwards, with an earlier article (2019) covering how the DPV was developed (open access versions here, here, and here).

Contributing: The DPVCG welcomes participation to improve the DPV and associated resources, including expansion or refinement of concepts, requesting information and applications, and addressing open issues. See contributing guide for further information.

A. Future Work

Issue 171: Provide consolidated list of legal basis, rights, and other relevant concepts for each jurisdiction rightstodolegal

E.g. in EU, there are rights, legal basis, etc. in GDPR, DGA, and so on. Provide a consolidated list of these in the EU extension (/legal/legal-eu.ttl) for convenience of use. A bunch of SPARQL queries over extensions should be able to do this.

Issue 387: Extension for the German GDNG legalreview

The German “Gesundheitsdatennutzungsgesetz” (GDNG) regulates many aspects of the use and access of health data in Germany. An extension for the EHDS is currently being worked on. Addressing national legislation at the same time would be very helpful for German initiatives such as the Medical Informatics Initiative (MII).
