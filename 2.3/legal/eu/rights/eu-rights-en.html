Abstract

The EU-RIGHTS extension provides concepts representing the EU Fundamental Rights for use as DataSubjectRights in Data Privacy Vocabulary (DPV) Specification. The namespace for terms in EU-RIGHTS is https://w3id.org/dpv/rights/eu#, the suggested prefix for the namespace is eu-rights, and the EU-RIGHTS vocabulary and its documentation is available on GitHub.

DPV Specifications: The [DPV] is the core specification that is extended by specific extensions. A [PRIMER] introduces the concepts and modelling of DPV specifications, and [GUIDES] describe application of DPV for specific applications and use-cases. The Search Index page provides a searchable hierarchy of all concepts. The Data Privacy Vocabularies and Controls Community Group (DPVCG) develops and manages these specifications through GitHub. For meetings, see the DPVCG calendar.

The peer-reviewed article "Data Privacy Vocabulary (DPV) - Version 2.0" (2024) describes the current state of DPV and extensions from version 2.0 onwards, with an earlier article (2019) covering how the DPV was developed (open access versions here, here, and here).

Contributing: The DPVCG welcomes participation to improve the DPV and associated resources, including expansion or refinement of concepts, requesting information and applications, and addressing open issues. See contributing guide for further information.

2. Impacts

The taxonomy of Impacts to EU Fundamental Rights in [EU-RIGHTS] extension takes each right within EU Charter of Fundamental Rights and creates a corresponding concept to represent an impact to that right. The modelling of impacts to rights is based on the [RISK] extension.

Note: Experimental granular impacts for Article 8 Data Protection

The following shows ongoing discussions regarding the expression of specific impacts with greater granularity. The discussion is based on the premise that we can express each right as a set of granular concepts, and then apply the specific rights impacts currently present in [RISK] extension over each to express how that particular facet of the right can be impacted. These concepts are experimental and may change in future versions.

The below concepts represent how Article 8 as a whole can be impacted:

  1. A8-Violated
  2. A8-ExercisePrevented
  3. A8-Limited
  4. A8-Denied
  5. A8-Unfulfilled
  6. A8-Obstructed
  7. A8-Eroded

The below concepts represent how specific aspects of Article 8 can be impacted:

  1. A8-UnfairProcessing
  2. A8-ProcessingWithNonspecificPurposes
  3. A8-ProcessingWithoutValidLegalBasis
  4. A8-ProcessingWithoutValidConsent
  5. A8-AccessImpacted
  6. A8-RectificationImpacted
Issue 210: [Concept]: Add granular Rights Impacts concepts rightsWIPhelp-wantedeu-gdpreu-rights

Specs

RISK, EU-GDPR, EU-Rights

New Concept(s)

See #184-comment with concepts proposed for each impact on right to be expressed as granular concepts:

  • RightDenied - denial that a right exists or applies
  • RightLimited - limit the scope of a right
  • RightUnfulfilled - unfulfilment of a right exercise
  • RightViolated - breach of a right in terms of its obligations, typically in a deliberate fashion
  • RightEroded - weakening of the right
  • RightObstructed - obstruction of the right or its exercise

And in addition to these, to interpret the right as a series of requirements or actions and create impacts based on that e.g. something is not fulfilled or something occurs (that wasn't supposed to). These concepts are proposed for EU-GDPR and EU-Rights extensions - but also for any rights concepts in DPV that can be accompanied by Rights Impact concepts.

  • RightLimited - limit the scope of a right
  • RightUnfulfilled - unfulfilment of a right exercise
  • RightViolated - breach of a right in terms of its obligations, typically in a deliberate fashion
  • RightEroded - weakening of the right
  • RightObstructed - obstruction of the right or its exercise

And in addition to these, to interpret the right as a series of requirements or actions and create impacts based on that e.g. something is not fulfilled or something occurs (that wasn't supposed to). These concepts are proposed for EU-GDPR and EU-Rights extensions - but also for any rights concepts in DPV that can be accompanied by Rights Impact concepts.
